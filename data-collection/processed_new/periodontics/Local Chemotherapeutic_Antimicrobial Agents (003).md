# Localized delivery of antimicrobial agents (003)

**Number:** 003
Update

**Subject:** Local delivery of antimicrobial agents (LDAs) as adjunctive therapy in the treatment of adult periodontitis

**Reviewed:** March 13, 2026

## Policy

Aetna considers the use of local antimicrobial agents as experimental and investigational in the following scenarios:

- When not delivered into diseased crevicular tissue, e.g. the teeth being treated exhibit no bone loss, or have periodontal probing depths of less than 5mm, or
- If there are multiple sites of recurrent and/or residual inflammation after periodontal scaling and root planing has been completed. When a generalized pattern of periodontitis is present, a more comprehensive intervention such as surgical therapy may be necessary.

Localized delivery of antimicrobial agents is not a covered service in most Aetna Dental plans. In plans where it is covered, localized delivery of antimicrobial agents is subject to strict clinical guidelines.

### Policy on Antimicrobial agents with implants

Antimicrobial agents such as Arestin have not been clinically tested for use in the regeneration of alveolar bone, either in preparation for or in conjunction with the placement of endosseous dental implants or in the treatment of failing implants. The use of antimicrobial agents such as Arestin in this application is considered by Aetna to be experimental and investigational.

## Background

Thorough Scaling &amp; Root Planing (SRP) is highly effective in the treatment of chronic periodontitis and is the standard approach to non-surgical periodontal therapy. In most cases, periodontal pockets will respond to SRP and require no additional adjunctive or surgical therapy. Recent data suggests that patients treated with SRP alone have clinically significant probing depth reductions, and locally applied antimicrobials such as minocycline microspheres were not shown to enhance these results.

In 2015, the Journal of the American Dental Association published the findings of a multidisciplinary panel convened by the American Dental Association (ADA) Council on Scientific Affairs (CSA.) The CSA presented clinical practice guidelines on the nonsurgical treatment of chronic periodontitis by means of SRP with or without adjuncts on the basis of a systematic review of the evidence. For patients with chronic periodontitis, the evidence-based review concluded SRP showed a moderate benefit, and the benefits were judged to outweigh potential adverse effects. The guidelines were in favor of SRP as the initial nonsurgical treatment for chronic periodontitis. Regarding adjuncts, however, the strength of recommendations for chlorhexidine chips was deemed to be "weak" and recommendations for doxycycline hyclate gel and minocycline microspheres were listed as "expert opinion for." (Note that "expert opinion for" does not imply endorsement but instead signifies that evidence is lacking and the level of certainty in the evidence is low.)

The ADA CSA evidence-based review could not definitively support the use of antimicrobial agents as adjuncts in conjunction with or following SRP. Therefore, since 2016, Aetna's clinical policy position is that local delivery of antimicrobials is not a covered service in most Aetna Dental plans.

CDT D4381 is not covered for members with a dental plan that became effective after October 1, 2016. Some Aetna members may still have coverage for D4381 if they are in a group dental plan or program that has not had a benefit modification since the 2016 policy change.

In plans where D4381 is a covered service, it must strictly adhere to these clinical criteria:

- Localized delivery of antimicrobial agents is used only to treat ***isolated sites*** of refractory crevicular disease after an adequate healing period of 6 weeks-6 months has occurred following the completion of scaling &amp; root planing (SRP.)
- Refractory disease sites are indicated by isolated periodontal pockets equal to or greater than 5 millimeters with persistent signs of inflammation, spontaneous bleeding or bleeding upon probing, suppuration and/or increasing loss of clinical attachment.
- Local delivery of chemotherapeutic agents may also be indicated when isolated refractory sites are diagnosed during a covered periodontal maintenance procedure.
- If there are ***multiple refractory sites*** or generalized residual inflammation after periodontal scaling and root planing has been completed, localized delivery of antimicrobial agents is not appropriate treatment, and a more comprehensive intervention such as surgical therapy may be necessary.
